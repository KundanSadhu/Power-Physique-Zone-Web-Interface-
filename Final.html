The Ultimate Guide to the Most Popular Bodybuilding Power Zone

Jeevan , karthik &Manikanta (co-founder's) Updated Mar 23, 2024.

Beginners (1 Month):

1. Monday (Chest & Triceps)

Working on the chest and triceps muscles together is efficient because many chest exercises (like bench presses) naturally engage the triceps as secondary muscles. Starting the week with a focus on these areas builds upper-body strength and pushes the limits on compound movements.

Chest Press: Builds strength in the chest, shoulders, and triceps.

Dumbbell Flyes: Isolates the chest muscles, creating a stretch for muscle growth.

Tricep Pushdowns: Targets the triceps to finish the workout with a specific focus.

2. Tuesday (Back & Biceps)

Back and bicep workouts are paired because many back exercises require pulling motions that also engage the biceps. This day helps balance upper-body strength and improves posture.

Lat Pulldowns: Focuses on the latissimus dorsi, enhancing back width.

Seated Cable Row: Works the middle of the back, supporting posture.

Dumbbell Bicep Curls: Isolates and builds the biceps for arm definition.

3. Wednesday (Rest)

Rest days are essential for muscle recovery and growth. Beginners benefit from spacing out workout days to prevent overuse injuries and give muscles time to rebuild.

4. Thursday (Shoulders & Abs)

This day focuses on shoulders and abs, which can support stability and strength in various other exercises. Working on abs also improves core strength and balance.

4. Friday (Back & Biceps)

Friday: Back and Biceps (Pull-Ups, Dumbbell Row, Bicep Curls, Face Pulls)

5. Saturday (Back & Biceps)

Saturday: Cardio and Full-Body Workout (Kettlebell Swings, Jump Rope, Burpees, Rowing Machine)

Sunday: Rest Day

3. Is Protein Good for Beginners?

Yes, protein is essential for muscle repair and growth. Beginners should aim for 1.2-1.5 grams of protein per kg of body weight.

4. Should We Take Pre-Protein or Post-Protein for Workouts?

Post-workout protein is generally more beneficial for muscle recovery. Pre-workout protein may be helpful if you feel fatigued before workouts.

5. What is the Diet for Bulking?

For effective bulking, add 300-500 extra calories per day. Aim for 35% protein, 45% carbohydrates, and 20% healthy fats.
